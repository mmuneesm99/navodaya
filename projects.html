The Creative Works

From landmark theatrical runs that introduced Cinemascope, 70mm, and stereoscopic 3D to India, to ongoing cross-platform script adaptations and biopics, explore the creative record of Navodaya.

Released · 1982

Padayottam

An epic historical revenge drama based on Alexandre Dumas' The Count of Monte Cristo. It is celebrated as India's first indigenous 70mm gauge film release, mixed and processed entirely within domestic laboratories.

View Archive Profile →
Released · 1984

My Dear Kuttichathan 3D

The trailblazing fantasy film that introduced stereoscopic cinema to the nation. Shot on a dual-strip 3D rig, it was later re-released in DTS digital audio format, breaking office records across India.

Script Stage · Historical Novel

Chundan

A deep socio-political, mathematical, and naval-architectural study of how the iconic snake boats (Chundan Vallam) of Keralam came to be. Written by Jijo, adapted from the original 2004 manuscript.

Proposed · Screenplay

D'Gama's Treasure (Barroz)

Based on the 2018 novel Barroz by Jijo, this proposed screenplay is set in Vasco da Gama's historical timeline, written in English and Hispanic languages for a global fantasy film adaptation.

Proposed · Costume Drama

CHEKON

A cinematic treatment of the northern ballots (Vadakkan Pattukal) focusing on the legend of Chandu Chekavar. Created by Jose Punnoose, establishing high-end choreographic and costume treatments.

Proposed · Native Folklore

Muthala

Set in the early 1900s in Kuttanad, this folklore recounts the conflict between a British surveyor (Sahib) and a legendary man-eating crocodile. Written by Jijo based on regional riverboat accounts.
