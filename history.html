The Studio History

Trailblazers in Indian Cinema

Under the stewardship of Maliampurackal Chacko Punnoose (Navodaya Appachan) and his sons Jijo and Jose, Navodaya pioneered the techniques, gauge sizes, and technologies that redefined Indian cinematography.

1950

The Foundation at Udaya Studios

Appachan joins his elder brother Kunchacko, the patriarch of Malayalam cinema, to establish Udaya Studios in Alappuzha. Together they would go on to produce nearly 100 films over a 25-year partnership, building the bedrock of the region's film industry.

M. Chacko Punnoose (Appachan) and Kunchacko working together on-set, circa 1960s.

1976

Navodaya Studio is Formed

Following Kunchacko's passing, Appachan establishes Navodaya Studio on the hills of Kakkanad in Kochi. Designed as an independent, state-of-the-art facility, the studio was envisioned from day one to serve as a hub of technological and creative innovation.

1978

Cinemascope & Outdoor Epics

Navodaya commissions Kadathanattu Makkam, shooting extensively in the rugged highlands of the Idukki reservoir rather than remaining enclosed on soundstages. Later that year, they launch Thacholi Ambu—the first anamorphic Cinemascope film in Malayalam cinema, establishing legendary actor Jayan as a star.

Cast and crew celebrating the massive commercial success and widescreen run of Thacholi Ambu (1978).

1982

Padayottam: The First Indian 70mm Film

Directed by Jijo Punnoose, Padayottam becomes the first movie in India to be shot and blown up entirely domestically to 70mm, matched with a 6-track stereophonic magnetic soundtrack. The studio surveyors inspect and modify theater designs state-wide to accommodate widescreen screens.

1984

My Dear Kuttichathan 3D

Released to standard acclaim, India's first 3D stereoscopic film is directed by Jijo. Navodaya designs its own projection rigs, handles distribution of red-and-cyan glasses, and equips theaters to align twin-lens setups to prevent projection convergence fatigue.

1992

The Non-Linear Editing Revolution

In a historic meeting, Jose Punnoose purchases the first Avid Non-Linear Digital Editing System and ProTools audio suite sold outside the United States. Navodaya establishes free editing workshops in Chennai and Kochi, training a generation of Bollywood and Kollywood editors to migrate away from mechanical Moviolas.

Theme Park Expansion

Kishkinta: India's First Theme Park

In 1995, building on their experience with cinematic spectacle and gravity illusions, Jose Punnoose and the Navodaya team opened Kishkinta in Chennai, spanning over 120 acres of high-end outdoor amusement rides, lakes, and attractions.
